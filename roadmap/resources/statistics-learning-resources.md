# Mean

## The mean represents the average value of a dataset and is one of the most commonly used measures in analytics.

### Formula:

Mean= ∑x / n
	​
### Example:

### Sales:
100, 150, 200, 250

### Mean:

(100+150+200+250)/4 = 175

## Business Application:

Average monthly sales
Average customer spending
Average order value
Median

### The median represents the middle value of an ordered dataset.

### Why analysts use median:

Less affected by outliers
Better for salary analysis

### Example:

5, 7, 10, 15, 100

### Median:
10

### Mode

### The mode is the most frequently occurring value.

### Business Applications:

Most sold product
Most common customer age group
Probability

### Probability measures the likelihood of an event occurring.

### Formula:

### P(A)= Favorable Outcomes / Total Outcomes

### Example:

### Probability of obtaining a Head when tossing a coin:

1/2 = 0.5

### Business Application:

Risk analysis
Customer churn prediction
